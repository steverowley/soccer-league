Sprite Studies·Motion construction

How the little men
should move

Stepping back from the arm tweaks. Pixel-sprite animation has its own conventions — and the reason the smooth rotating arms felt off is that they fight those conventions. Below: the principles, then three ways to build the figure's motion, each animating live. Pick a direction and I'll commit to it.

What the research says
ISilhouette first. At sprite scale, readability beats realism — the pose has to read when you squint. Exaggerate; don't smooth.
IIA walk is four poses. Contact, passing, contact, passing — opposite arm and leg extended, a slight forward lean. Not a continuous rotation.
IIIAnimate "on 2s". Holding each pose for a beat reads weightier and more deliberate than a new position every frame. Smoothness is what makes ours float.
IVIdles are cheap. A two-frame bob and the occasional flourish. Over-bob and they look like they're on the moon.
Three directions·same figures, different rigs
A

Procedural rig

Joints rotate continuously — what we have now. Maximally flexible and smooth, and the arm-flail tweaks live here. The catch the research predicts: continuous rotation "on 1s" reads a touch floaty and un-pixel, like a posable toy rather than a drawn sprite.smooth · flexible · can feel floaty
B

Pose-to-pose

Recommended
The authentic approach: a small set of exaggerated key poses, snapped and held "on 2s". Arms swing as a pair opposite the legs in a clear stride. Snappier, weightier, unmistakably a pixel sprite — and it reads cleanly even tiny on the pitch.snappy · readable · retro-correct
C

Chibi nub

Strip the arms to fixed stubby nubs and let the body bob and squash carry all the life — the Blaseball / Tiny-Terraces minimalist read. No arm articulation to get wrong, maximum charm, the most "on-brand austere" of the three.clean · cute · least fussy

My pick: B, Pose-to-pose. It's what makes pixel sprites feel like pixel sprites, it stays legible at pitch scale, and it ends the arm-flail rabbit hole. Say the word and I'll make it the default in the main file (it's already wired as the Limbs → Pose tweak there).
