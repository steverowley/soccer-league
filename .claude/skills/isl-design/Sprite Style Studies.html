Not the rig this time — the whole character. A pixel-sprite style is a locked spec: resolution, palette size, one outline rule, a shading model, and proportions. Below are the principles, then the same ISL footballer built to five different specs. Same silhouette, five identities — pick one and I'll make it the house style.

The style spec — what we'd lock
IOutline rule. Hard black, hue-shifted ("sel-out"), or none — pick one and never mix. It's the single biggest driver of era and mood.
IIShading model. Flat (1 tone), cel (base + shadow + highlight), or dithered. 2–3 tones per part, ~6–12 per figure.
IIIProportions. 2-head chibi reads friendliest at tiny sizes; 2.5–3 heads feels more athletic. Consistency across the roster matters more than the number.
IVSilhouette & value. It must read in pure black, and survive desaturation. Kit colour stays the one accent so the brand holds.
Five directions·one footballer, five specs

My pick: Phosphor cel. It's the only one that's unmistakably ISL rather than generic-retro — Lunar Dust values, Galactic-Abyss outline, kit as the single rationed accent — while the cel shading gives it real craft and weight. Flat-8bit is the safe fallback; Sel-out modern if we want it softer and more premium.
